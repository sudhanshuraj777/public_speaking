Overcoming Stage Fright

Why Do We Feel Nervous?

Stage fright, or glossophobia, happens because our brain perceives public speaking as a threat. The body's fight-or-flight response kicks in, causing sweating, shaky hands, and a racing heart.

Techniques to Reduce Anxiety:

Deep Breathing: Try the 4-7-8 technique—inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8.

Visualization: Imagine yourself giving a successful speech.

Practice & Preparation: The more you rehearse, the more confident you all feel.

Positive Self-Talk: Replace “I will mess up” with “I am prepared and ready.”

Start Small: Practice in front of a mirror, then with friends, then a small audience.

How to Structure an Impactful Speech A well-structured speech ensures clarity and engagement.

Three Key Parts of a Speech:

Introduction (15%)

Hook the audience with a story, question, or quote.
Introduce the topic in a compelling way.
Provide a preview of what you’ll cover.

Body (70%)

Use the "Rule of Three"—present 3 main points.
Include examples, statistics, and stories for each point.
Transition smoothly between ideas.

Conclusion (15%)

Summarize key points concisely.
End with a powerful statement or call to action.

Using Body Language to Enhance Your Speech

Why Body Language Matters:

Your words make up only 7% of communication, while body language and voice tone contribute 93%!

Essential Body Language Tips:

Eye Contact: Engage different sections of the audience instead of staring at one spot.
Gestures: Use open hand gestures for confidence. Avoid crossing arms (it looks defensive).
Posture: Stand tall with shoulders back to exude confidence.
Movement: Avoid pacing. Move purposefully to emphasize points.
Facial Expressions: Match your emotions with your speech.

The Art of Storytelling in Public Speaking

Why Stories Work:

Stories capture attention, create emotions, and make your message memorable.

How to Craft a Compelling Story:

Set the Scene: Describe where and when it happened.
Introduce a Conflict: What problem or challenge was faced?
Build Suspense: Keep the audience engaged with curiosity.
Resolve with a Lesson: End with a meaningful takeaway.

Mastering Vocal Variety for Impact

Why Vocal Variety Matters:

A monotone voice can make even the best speech sound dull.

Techniques for Vocal Variety:

Pitch: Use a higher pitch for excitement and a lower one for authority.
Pace: Speed up for energy, slow down for emphasis.
Pauses: A well-timed pause creates suspense and lets key points sink in.
Volume: Raise your voice to show confidence; lower it for dramatic effect.

Handling Q&A Like a Pro

Golden Rules for Handling Questions:

Listen Carefully: Don’t interrupt. Nod to show engagement.
Repeat the Question (If Necessary): This helps clarify and gives you time to think.
Stay Calm and Confident: If you don’t know an answer, say, “That’s an interesting question! Let me look into it and get back to you.”
Be Brief and Relevant: Stick to the point to keep the audience engaged.
End with a Thank You:“Great question! Thanks for bringing that up.”

How to Memorize a Speech Without Sounding Robotic

Memory Techniques for Public Speaking:

Chunking Method: Break your speech into small, easy-to-remember sections.
Mind Palace Technique: Associate key points with places in a familiar location.
Keyword Trigger Method: Memorize only key phrases rather than full sentences.
Practice Out Loud: Reading silently isn’t enough—verbal practice is key.
Use Visual Aids:A slide or simple cue card can jog your memory.
